This on-screen keyboard is designed for typing in S'gaw Karen, a language spoken in Thailand and Myanmar. Many S'gaw Karen people use "lix wa" characters to write their language, which are included in the Myanmar Unicode range. However, common Burmese fonts and keyboards often do not support the additional characters that are used in S'gaw Karen, which makes it very hard to communicate in Karen online. By typing in this on-screen keyboard, S'gaw Karen users can write their language using the proper Unicode codepoints, no downloads required.

How to use the on-screen keyboard : Set your keyboard to English (QWERTY), click inside the text area above, and start typing. Or, just click on the letter you want. You can then copy and paste your text into another program.
